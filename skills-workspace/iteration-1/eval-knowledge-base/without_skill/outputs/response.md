# What to Study Next

Based on your current progress, you should study **Multi-Layer Networks** next.

Here's why:

- You've mastered **Linear Algebra Basics** and **Gradient Descent** — these are exactly the two prerequisites for Multi-Layer Networks.
- **Backpropagation** depends on Multi-Layer Networks, so you need to tackle networks first before backprop will make sense.
- Once you finish Multi-Layer Networks, Backpropagation becomes your natural next step.

## Suggested order going forward:

1. **Multi-Layer Networks** (start here — all prerequisites met)
2. **Backpropagation** (after completing Multi-Layer Networks)

If you want to work on something in parallel while studying Multi-Layer Networks, you could continue with **Probability Theory** (currently in-progress, no dependencies blocking it).

## Why not Backpropagation first?

Backpropagation requires understanding how information flows through multi-layer networks. Jumping straight to backprop without that foundation typically leads to confusion about *what* is being propagated and *why*.

Go tackle Multi-Layer Networks — your existing knowledge of linear algebra and gradient descent will make it very approachable.
