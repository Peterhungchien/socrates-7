## Subject Being Studied

**Deep Learning** — starting from theoretical foundations: computation graphs,
backpropagation, optimization landscapes, and core architectures (MLPs, CNNs, attention).

---

## Current Knowledge Level

- Strong mathematical foundation: linear algebra, real analysis, measure-theoretic
  probability, convex optimization.
- Practical experience: has trained models in PyTorch and sklearn for coursework and
  research projects. Can get a network to converge.
- Theoretical gap: has not formally derived backpropagation, has not studied computation
  graph theory, has only informal intuitions for batch normalization, attention, and
  depth-vs-width tradeoffs.
- Has not connected statistical learning theory (PAC learning, generalization bounds)
  to modern deep learning phenomena.

---

## Learning Goals

1. Build rigorous theoretical understanding of deep learning from first principles —
   not just to use it, but to know *why* it works.
2. Derive backpropagation cleanly from the chain rule on computation graphs.
3. Understand core architectural choices (CNNs, attention, normalization) with
   mathematical justification, not just empirical motivation.
4. Develop the ability to read DL research papers critically and independently.
5. Identify where and how DL methods can be incorporated into statistical research
   with rigor.
